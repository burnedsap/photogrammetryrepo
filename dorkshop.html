Hardware Requirements

All you need is a smartphone of a 2014 vintage or better, and a half-decent laptop.

Also, a fast and reliable way of transferring photos from your smartphone to a computer.

You can of course use a proper camera, and the results will definitely be much better, but unless you're creating 4K ready assets for a AAA game or VFX production, a smartphone is really all you need. It's also much quicker to process photos from a smartphone, so you'll get to a textured, 3D model much faster.

The same goes for a computer. You can definitely get away with using a machine like mine (Macbook Air from 2014 with 4GB RAM), but you'll be much better off with of a faster machine. Photogrammetry is a real stress-test for any computer, and requires a great CPU, a great GPU, lots of (fast) RAM, and plenty of disk space.

Windows based machines are preferred, but other systems such are quickly getting up to speed. Linux support is very good, and the Mac now has native photogrammetry libraries.

Software Requirements

There's a whole bunch of options available but there's a few I personally recommend.

Reality Capture: (Windows and Linux) The industry standard, it's one of the best out there. It's paid, but they have a cheap trial via Steam, and it works well with Nvidia GPU's. Highly recommend.

Meshroom: (Windows and Linux) A free and open-source option, this is apparently very good. I haven't personally used it, but lots of people have made lots of approving noises about it. It's free, so no harm in trying it out.

Metashape: (MacOS, Windows and Linux) A great option for Mac users, it's also available for other platforms. It leverages Metal, the Mac GPU framework, so it's fairly efficient and fast. Highly recommend for anyone who uses a Mac.

The Ten Commandments

Be patient. This will take time.

Not everything will work, but you won't be the first person to face your issue. Someone, somewhere has probably had the same issue, and the solution is probably online.

Learn the rules of photogrammetry: It will make everything faster.

Break the rules of photogrammetry: Once you know what works, break the rules. There's plenty of creative opportunities with this workflow
